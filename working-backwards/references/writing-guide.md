# Writing Guide — Narrative Best Practices for PR/FAQs

## Core Principles

### 1. Customer-Centric Language

Drop business speak. Write in language a real customer would use.

| Bad (internal jargon) | Good (customer language) |
|-----------------------|--------------------------|
| "Uses machine learning and quality scoring to optimise content" | "When you use the app, everything you see feels relevant to you" |
| "Leverage our proprietary AI framework" | "Get instant answers trained on your tutor's teaching style" |
| "Omnichannel commerce solution" | "Buy tickets on our website or app" |

### 2. Eliminate Weasel Words

Every vague word has a specific replacement:

| Weasel word | Replace with |
|-------------|-------------|
| Nearly all customers | 7.6M customers |
| Huge improvement | +25 basis points |
| Significant increase | 42% increase (p<0.01) |
| Many users | 340,000 users |
| Most platforms | 8 of 12 platforms |
| Recently | On 15 March 2026 |
| Soon | By Q3 2026 |
| Very fast | 30ms response time |
| Affordable | £5.99/month |

### 3. Sentence Length: Under 30 Words

| Bloated | Concise |
|---------|---------|
| Due to the fact that | Because |
| Totally lacked the ability to | Could not |
| In order to | To |
| At this point in time | Now |
| In the event that | If |
| A large number of | Many / [specific number] |
| Has the capability to | Can |

### 4. Replace Adjectives with Data

| Adjective-heavy | Data-driven |
|-----------------|-------------|
| We made desktop much faster | We reduced time-to-interactive p90 latency from 200ms to 30ms |
| The platform has grown rapidly | Monthly active users grew from 12,000 to 89,000 in 6 months |
| Prices are very competitive | Our £7.99/month is 85% cheaper than the market average of £52/hour |
| Customer satisfaction is high | NPS increased from 34 to 67; CSAT at 4.6/5.0 |

### 5. The "So What" Test

After every sentence, ask: "So what? Why does the customer care?"

If you can't answer that, cut the sentence or rewrite it with the customer benefit.

### 6. One Problem Per PR/FAQ

If your press release addresses multiple distinct problems, split into separate PR/FAQs. An umbrella PR/FAQ can show how a suite of solutions solves an overarching problem, but each component needs its own document.

### 7. Information Hierarchy

Think of the reader as someone who reads from the top and may stop at any point. Every additional sentence must add the next most important information and make sense without anything that comes after it.

### 8. Make the Reader Empathise

Reading the problem description should make us question why we do this to our customers and create excitement to fix it. Don't just state the problem — make it felt.

### 9. The Solution Can't Be Magic

Don't propose something that can't be done. You need at least a high-level idea of the entire solution. If the solution requires new content, processes, or partnerships — who does it, how, and does it affect the timeline?

### 10. Reference Other Documents with Care

Expect that readers have no time to click links. Don't add any in the press release body. Only add links in the FAQ, and only when you need to reference specific research or competitor experiences.

## Formatting Standards

### Basic Formatting Requirements

| Element | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| Document type | Word (.docx) or Google Docs |
| Paper size | A4 |
| Margins | Narrow — 1.25cm left/right, 1.25cm top/bottom |
| Font | Arial or Calibri, minimum 10pt |
| Line spacing | Single |
| Footer | "[Company] Confidential" centred; Page X of Z right-aligned |
| FAQ numbering | Auto-numbered, sequential across all sections |
| Length | Press release ≤1 page; FAQs 2-5 pages; Total ≤6 pages (excl. appendix) |

### Document Header

Include at the top:
- Document title
- Date
- Author(s)
- Contributors
- Status (Draft / Review / Approved)

### Heading Hierarchy

- **Document title**: Bold, larger font
- **Section headers** (Press Release, External FAQs, Internal FAQs, Appendix): Bold, underlined
- **Sub-section headers**: Bold
- **FAQ questions**: Bold, numbered

## Grammar and Style Rules

### Voice

Use **active voice**. Avoid passive constructions.

| Passive (bad) | Active (good) |
|---------------|---------------|
| A process to monitor repeat contacts was created | The Red team implemented a process to monitor repeat contacts |
| The platform will be launched in Q3 | We launch the platform in Q3 |
| Customers are provided with tools | Customers receive tools |

### Perspective by Section

| Section | Person |
|---------|--------|
| Press release narrative | Third person |
| Leader quote | First person (quoted) |
| Customer testimonial | First person (quoted) |
| External FAQs | Second person ("you", "your") |
| Internal FAQs | First person plural ("we", "our") |
| Appendix | Third person / neutral |

### Numbers

- Spell out one to nine
- Use integers from 10 to 999
- Use capital letters for thousands and above: 1K, 1M, 1B
- Hyphenate number-noun compounds: "two-day", "14-day"
- Use £ / $ with M for money: £10M, $24.99
- Percentages: use % symbol (not "percent")
- Percentage changes: "a two percentage point increase" (not "2% increase") or use basis points

### Dates

- Use sortable format: YYYY-MM-DD (e.g., 2026-03-30)
- Or written format for press release: "30th March 2026"
- Spell out months in press release body

### Abbreviations

Spell out all abbreviations on first mention with abbreviation in parentheses. Use abbreviation thereafter.

Example: "Customer Data Platform (CDP)" then "CDP" for the rest of the document.

### Commonly Confused Words

| Word | Usage |
|------|-------|
| affect / effect | Affect = influence (verb); Effect = result (noun) |
| its / it's | Its = possessive; It's = "it is" |
| there / their / they're | There = place; Their = possessive; They're = "they are" |
| comprise / consist | "Comprise" never takes "of" — use "consist of" or "comprise" alone |

### Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|-------------|
| Impactful | Influential / significant / [describe the impact] |
| Leverage (as verb) | Use |
| Utilise | Use |
| Synergy | [Describe the specific benefit] |
| Cutting-edge | [Describe what makes it new] |
| World-class | [Provide the benchmark] |
| Innovative | [Describe the innovation] |
| Disrupting | [Describe what changes] |
| Firstly, secondly | First, second |

## The Review Process

### Timeline

A good PR/FAQ typically takes:
- **2+ weeks** before a first draft is ready for review
- **1+ month** for approval after iterative reviews
- The document evolves with feedback and new insights

### Running a Review Meeting

1. **Schedule**: 60 minutes minimum. Include video/call bridge. Distribute document in advance
2. **Pre-read**: Remind participants to bring laptops for commenting
3. **Silent reading**: First 20-30 minutes — participants read and add comments directly in the document
4. **Page-by-page review**: Walk through feedback page by page after silent reading
5. **Capture everything**: Write all feedback as comments in the document, even if not incorporated
6. **Confirm next steps**: Revise and re-review? Move to design? Begin development?

### Before Sharing: Review Checklist

- Is it clear who the customer is?
- Does it state the problem that exists?
- Is the single most important benefit clear?
- Does it paint a compelling picture of the solution?
- Does it reinforce value via a customer testimonial?
- Are all FAQs answered with data?
- Are trade-offs presented with options?
- Would someone unfamiliar with the project fully understand the goal?
- Do you buy your own story?

## Tips for Writing Quotes

### Leader Quote

The leader quote should:
- Capture the single most important value proposition
- Feel visionary but grounded in specifics
- Be attributable to a real named person with their title
- Not repeat what's already in the press release — add new perspective

**Example:**
> "Customers who purchase Ultra HD TVs know they need a source for Ultra HD content to complete their experience," said Bill Carr, Vice President of Movies and Music at Amazon. "We're excited to offer them a convenient way to watch great quality Ultra HD movies and TV shows."

### Customer Testimonial

The testimonial should:
- Include a specific name and location/role
- Describe a concrete, believable scenario
- Quantify the benefit where possible
- Sound like a real person talking, not marketing copy
- Reinforce the key benefit through personal experience

**Example:**
> "My daughter subscribes to three tutors for her GCSEs — Maths, Chemistry, and English — for less than we used to pay for one hour of traditional tutoring," says James Mitchell, parent from Birmingham. "She's more engaged than ever because it feels like following her favourite YouTubers, except she's actually learning."
